The public transportation in Berlin is safe and affordable. The city has an ectensive network of trains, underground trains, buses and trams. Berlin has also a really good cycling infrastructure and in many parts of the city there is an extensive cycle path network.

Ticket Fares

One Way Ticket:
A single fare ticket (Einzelfahrschein) is valid for two hour trip and it costs 2.90 Euros (Tariff AB), 3.30 Euros (Tarriff BC), 3.60 Euros (Tariff ABC). There is also a posibility to purchase a reduced price ticket.

Short Distance Ticket:
A short distance ticket (Kurzstrecke) is valid for three stops with S- and U-Bahn and or six stops on buses and trams and costs 1.90 Euros. It's important to note that with this ticket it's allowed to change the trains, but not buses or trams.

Tickets for Children:
Children up to the age of five can travel without any charge. Children from 6 to 14 years can pursue the reduced tariff tickets.

Monthly Tickets:
The monthly ticket is valid 1 month from the day of purchase or or other date selected. Monthly ticket for Berlin AB zone costs 86.00 Euros.

Yearly Abonament:
The yearly transport ticket costs 60.67 Euros per month. The yearly BVG subscription can be purchased online or in BVG's Customer Centers (Kundenzentren).

Buying and Validating Tickets

Tickets can be purchased at ticket machines on the platforms of S-and U-Bahn stations. In trams there are tickets machines inside the trains and in buses fares are paid to the bus driver. In some bigger stations you tickets can be purchases at BVG sales counters.Ticket has to be validated before the journey starts. To validate tickets you need to stamp them at the yellow or red machines on the platforms, in buses or trams.

For information please visit Berlin official website or BVG website.
