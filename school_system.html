How does the Berlin school system work?

All children in Germany must attend school for at least 9 years. In Berlin, all children older than 10 have to go to school. Attending school is free.
Have a look at the Homepage for the Senate Administration for Education, Youth and Family Affairs for an overview of school system in Berlin.

Primary school (Grundschule)

In Berlin all children from the age of 6 go to primary school (or international school). Primery school lasts 6 years and after that period children according to their skills change to an integrated secondary school or high school. Children who go to an interdenominational school have the option to stay there after the primary school period.
Find out more about primery schools in Berlin here.

Secondary school (Gymnasium)

Secondary school lasts usually 6 years and and allows students to take the final examination (the Abutur) after. Many Gymnasium have a specific focus, for example, scientific, musical, pedagogical or linguistic.

Integrated secondary school (Sekundarschule)

The Sekundarschule is a comprehensive school and is attended by students with a wide range of abilities. Based on how well they perform in school, pupils graduating from a Sekundarschule can receive the Hauptschulabschluss, the Realschulabschluss or the Abitur.
