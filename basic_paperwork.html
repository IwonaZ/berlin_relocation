Arriving to Germany might feel overwhelming at first. The amount of paperwork you need to do is quite impressive, but German bureaucracy is manageable, you just need the right resources and support. Below is the list of the first steps that you might need to do in order to have a great start in your new life in Berlin.

Visa

Visa in needed for entering Germany in many cases. You can apply for it thourough German embassy in your own country, but keep in mind the application process can take approximately two to three months. All information on visa requirements can be found on the website of the German Foreign Affairs Office .

If you are from the Member Statesof the European Union (EU), Switzerland, Iceland, Norway and Liechtenstein, you can enter Germany with just a valid passport or ID card.

Registration

You have to register with the Residents’ Registration Office (Bürgeramt) within 2 weeks of the arrival date in Germany. Registration offices in Berlin are really busy, so it's usually not a problem if you register a bit later.

Which Bürgeramt will be responsible for your registration depends on in which district of Berlin you are living. Find out where the Bürgeramt of your district is located on Berlin’s official website.

Tax ID

The Tax ID (Steueridentifikationsnummer) is an 11 digit number issued by the German Federal Tax Office that identifies you. This number is unique and will serve you for the whole duration of your time in Germany.

The Tax Office will send you the the tax ID automatically to your registered address withing 4 weeks after your registration at Bürgeramt.

If you want to work in Germany, you need to provide this number to your employer, otherwise the maximum tax rate will be deducted from your income.

Bank Account

Your salary must be transferred by your employer into a German bank account. You can choose whatever bank you like.

Some of the big traditional banks in Germany include Deutsche Bank, Sparkasse, Postbank, Commerzbank, Deutsche Kreditbank, and Berliner Volksbank.

Online banks without physical offices include N26, DKB, and ING-DiBa.

Health Insurance

Health insurance is mandatory for everyone in Germany. You can choose whatever insurance you like (either statutory health insurance or private health insurance) and register online easily. Your membership certificate will be sent to you via post. Here are more details on how to choose German health insurance.

Other Insurances

To insure yourself against different risks, you should purchase an insurance policy. The most popular kinds of insurances in Germany are:

  • Liability Insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung)
  • Motor Vehicle Insurance (Kfz-Versicherung)
  • Household Insurance (Hausratversicherung)
  • Accidental Death and Disablement Insurance (Unfallversicherung)
  • Life Insurance (Lebensversicherung)
  • Legal Insurance (Rechtsschutzversicherung)
  • Pet health insurance (Tierkrankenversicherung)
  • Travel Insurance (Reiseversicherung)
